In order to use Rewind, you will have to have location history data from Google Maps. If you’d like to enable location tracking, click here to do so. If you already have tracking enabled, click on the right arrow ( > ) to learn how to retrieve your data!

To exit this tutorial, just click on the X button at the top right at any time.

2 / 6
Step 1:

Click here and sign-in to the Google account associated with your location history. You should be taken to this page:

3 / 6
Step 2:

Click on the Settings icon in the bottom right hand corner. Select ‘Download a copy of all your data.’

4 / 6
Step 3:

Select ‘Location History,’ and make sure that ‘JSON’ is selected as shown below.

If there are other Google products shown on this page, be sure to deselect all of them. You can do this by clicking ‘Select none,’ as shown below. Make sure that ‘Location History’ is selected after doing this.

Click next once you are finished.

5 / 6
Step 4:

Choose ‘.zip’ for file type and ‘2GB’ for archive size. You may choose the delivery method that works best for you.

After you click ‘Create archive,’ your JSON file will be delivered to you by your chosen method as a zip file.

6 / 6
Step 5:

Open your downloaded zip file to retrieve the JSON file needed for Rewind. Double click on the .zip file to unzip it, and navigate to the “Location History” folder. Your Location History data resides in this folder!

You can simply click and drag this JSON file into Rewind.

You’re all set!
